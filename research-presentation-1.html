üî¨ 3. Scientific & Social Value

Recent research focuses on three major directions:

  • Algorithmic Transparency and Bias
    Exploring whether systems amplify certain genres or demographic patterns
  • Personalization-Diversity Trade-off

‚úÖ Selected Research Questions

Question 1: First-Song Effect

How does the genre or audio features of the first selected songs affect the recommended songs genres?

Minimum Viable Features (MVP):

  • Starting song features: track_id, artist_id, genre
  • Audio features: valence, energy, danceability, tempo, acousticness, instrumentalness, loudness

Question 2: User Personalization

How does user information such as age and gender affect the recommended songs?

Minimum Viable Features (MVP):

  • User profile: age, gender (simulated via test accounts)
  • Recommendation outputs: track_id, artist_id, genre, audio features

‚ùå Removed Questions

Device Type & Account Type

Question: Does recommendation differ by device (mobile, desktop, speaker) or account type (Free vs. Premium)?

Reason for removal:

  • Manual data collection required for mobile and smart speakers
  • Time and energy consuming
  • No resources for premium accounts

Contextual Factors

Question: Do time of day, weekday, or special occasions affect recommendations?

Reason for removal:

  • Increases study complexity
  • Introduces uncontrollable confounding variables (mood, location)
  • Limited data collection period

‚ùå Removed Questions (continued)

Social Network Influence

Question: Does a user's social network or friends' listening behavior influence their Spotify recommendations?

Reason for removal:

  • Greatly increases workload (requires more accounts)
  • Artificial friendships between test accounts lack authenticity
  • Missing: messaging, long-term history, interactive behavior
  • Cannot reflect real user social interactions
